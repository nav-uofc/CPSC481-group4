Introduction

Our group has decided to design a study group finder web application to give students and tutors a platform to collaborate. In today’s fast-paced world, everyone is busy and there are few opportunities for spontaneous face to face interactions. Therefore, there is a market for an app to help students connect with study partners. Our application is intended to fill this niche: it will allow students to form or join study groups, rate and review peers, and collaborate through chat service. We intend for the platform we provide to aid any student in achieving academic success. The system will be accessed via a web browser from any computer/device with an internet connection. High school students, college/university students, and others can join and use the platform.

Research

  • A chat channel for a community dedicated to “rationalist” thought.
  • An internet forum primarily for writers of science fiction.
  • A chat channel for a community of writers and programmers.
  • A chat channel for tabletop roleplayers.
  • A chat channel for a group house in San Francisco’s Bay Area.
  • The Slack channel for the class this project is being done on.
  • One of the authors’ personal Facebook pages.

Survey Findings

  • The majority of people don’t use an app or software to find study buddies.
  • People did not know about a service/application that helps people to find study groups
  • Majority don’t use the service of a tutor
  • Majority was willing to use a group finder app. Since many don’t know any other similar platform, they could be interested in trying the app.
  • Majority thought direct messaging and file sharing features were highly important.
  • Majority thinks peer & tutor ratings are important.
  • Open-ended questions were more useful in some ways, because they gave us more access to individual opinions about how studying in groups works or doesn’t work for people. However, they were more likely to not be answered at all, or answered uselessly.
  • In general, those polled don’t prefer to study in groups.
  • Multiple people polled are introverted and dislike meeting strangers.
  • Multiple people polled feel that their peers are poor at communal study.
  • It’s possible to get multiple-submission errors with google forms, which can skew results. Hand checking is necessary.
  • There was a remarkably low return rate, given the number of places the survey was posted. However, it was not as low as we had feared - we expected “maybe five responses”, but in fact got 20 valid returns.

Survey Reflection

The survey questions allowed us to learn a lot about how many people would actually use the application and how/when they would use it. Using open ended questions gave us a lot of information from people completing the survey and turned out to be very useful. Unfortunately, we had a very small amount of people actually complete the survey so, even though we received very useful information from those people, we were unable to get a very wide range of viewpoints regarding the application.

Pros and cons of using an online app to connect with people?

Mujeeb: convenience, and helps look at their background before meeting.

Interview Findings & Reflection

The interview questions have helped us to better understand when a student may use the application and that even though not all people would be interested in using it there are a number of students who would. It has also taught us the potential good and bad things about the service as well as what could be added to make it better. There were no privacy/security concerns we have learned beyond what we already expected. The questions, however, seemed a bit too narrow and if we had asked more in depth questions we could have received information that would have given us a better starting point for starting to create the product.

Task Descriptions

Task 1: Student forming a study group

Lewys is a university student. He is very social and learns best in groups. Math 211 has been a bit difficult for him and the final exam is coming up. He has got together with two of his close friends taking the same course to study together, but there were still many parts that they weren’t sure of. Lewys decides to form a larger study group for the final exam review so they could cover as much course material across as many people as possible. He creates a final exam review study group on the study group finder app. While setting up the group, he sets the institution to his university and sets the course to his math course. Finally, he gives several times slots for when he’s available. A few hours later, he receives an email from the site informing him that someone has joined his group and matched with several of his time slots.

Task 2: Student struggling and in need of help

Simon is a university student in the same math course as Lewys. He is currently struggling with the course and is behind on lots of course material. Final exam is approaching, and this is a course he cannot afford to fail. When he is stuck, he usually searches on the internet for answers but learning by himself from the internet takes some time and he is too far behind on the course to cover enough material before the final. He decides he needs a quicker way to learn and thinks about joining a study group. He searches his university and his math course on the study group finder app and sees that there is a final review group for his course. He decides to join the group. Once he joins, he selects the time slots already available that also work with his schedule.

Task 3: Wants to join the group but time doesn’t fit

Amy is a university student in the same math course as Lewys and Simon. She thinks she is doing well in the course but would like to compare her knowledge with other students in the same course to feel confident about her grasp of the course material. She thinks joining Lewys’ study group would be beneficial for her review of the course. However, the meeting times already suggested for Lewys’ group do not fit with her schedule. She joins the study group and sends a message in the group chat to negotiate an alternative meeting time with the group members.

Task 4: Tutor new to the system, whose goal is to earn money

Harper is working many part time jobs but still needs extra cash.  She decides tutoring high school students would be a good way to earn extra cash. She signs up on the study group finder app. Since she is new to the study group finder app, she doesn’t have any ratings as a tutor on the app.  Since this is her first time tutoring, her rating begins at 2.5.        

Task 5: High school student needing a tutor

Cynthia is a high school student. She wants to get into a certain university so getting good grades is important to her. She is looking for a tutor but cannot afford much. She talks to her friend Heather, another high school student who would like a tutor, and they use Cynthia’s account on the study group finder to create a group. Cynthia sends Heather an invite to the group. Heather receives the invite via an email containing a link to the site. Heather does not have an account, so she is redirected to the sign up page, and creates one. There is a notification with Cynthia’s invitation waiting for her. She accepts the invitation. Neither of them enjoy working in large groups, so Cynthia closes the group to other students. They decide to divide the payment equally between them, but since they still cannot afford to pay too much, they look for an inexpensive tutor.

Task 6: Parent looking for a tutor for their child

Nicholas is a parent who is very concerned with the education of his daughter Leanna, a junior high student. Leanna struggles to study on her own but is very introverted, has trouble making friends at school, and does not enjoy working in large groups. Nicholas creates an account in his daughter’s name, using his own email, and searches the study group finder for small junior high level study groups with tutors. He finds an open group with a very highly rated tutor and applies to join it. The tutor receives an email notifying that another student has joined the open group.
